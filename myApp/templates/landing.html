The Insight
The problem
isn't knowledge.
It's execution.

Public health systems don't fail because people don't know what to do. They fail because behaviour is not properly targeted, systems are not aligned, execution is too slow, and communication doesn't lead to action. To solve this, you don't need more information. You need a system.

01
Behaviour is not properly targeted

Without a structured approach to behaviour design, campaigns create awareness without action. Good intentions aren't enough.

02
Systems are not aligned

When leadership, teams, and structures pull in different directions, even the best plans fail at the execution stage.

03
Execution is too slow

Decision bottlenecks and coordination failures slow programs to a crawl. Speed of execution is a strategic capability.

04
Communication doesn't lead to action

Most health communication is designed to inform. But informing people is not the same as changing their behaviour.
